The Right Guitar Pedal Chain

The order of your pedals (your signal chain) can dramatically affect your overall tone. Here's a comprehensive guide to creating the perfect pedal chain.

The Standard Pedal Order

While there's room for experimentation, here's the traditional order that works for most situations:

1. Tuner

Place your tuner first to get the cleanest signal for accurate tuning. It should also have a bypass feature to mute your signal when tuning.

2. Dynamics/Filters

  • Wah pedal
  • Compressor
  • EQ
  • Volume pedal (can also go last)

3. Gain Stages

  • Overdrive
  • Distortion
  • Fuzz

Tip: Arrange from lowest to highest gain for the most versatile sound options.

4. Modulation

  • Chorus
  • Flanger
  • Phaser
  • Tremolo

5. Time-Based Effects

  • Delay
  • Reverb

Tip: Reverb usually goes last to create a natural space for all other effects.

Signal Chain Considerations

  • Buffer placement (start and end of chain)
  • Cable length and quality
  • Power supply isolation
  • True bypass vs. buffered bypass

Common Variations

  • Volume pedal can go before or after drive pedals
  • Wah can go after drive for a different character
  • Some players prefer modulation before drive
  • Parallel effects loops for more complex setups

Signal Chain Tips

  • Keep cable runs short
  • Use isolated power supplies
  • Consider stereo effects last
  • Test different combinations

Troubleshooting

  • Signal loss: Check cables and buffers
  • Noise: Check power supplies
  • Tone loss: Consider buffer placement
  • Ground loops: Use isolated power
